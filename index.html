My thoughts on CS education

Computers and the internet are the latest technological revolution humans have created, and many have agreed that these tools have a huge positive impact on human life. The combination of both has immensely improved communication around the world and the processing of data resulting in new inventions in medicine, agriculture, commerce, transportation, science, finance, and education, among many others.

I am a firm believer that computer science education in grades K-12 is vital in today's digital world because it provides students with the necessary knowledge and skills to develop creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving that are important not only in this subject, but they also help students to stimulate their attention and improve their performance in other subjects such as math and physics. Moreover, those skills prepare students for their professional life.

Since computer science is a relatively new area in children's education, it is still limited in schools. However, I think t it will change in the short temp. For instance, over the last few years, I have seen New York willing to make Computer Science accessible to students of all school ages. Schools should definitely make efforts to ensure that all students have equal opportunities to access, learn and succeed in this field.

Introduction to programming in Python

This unit introduces text-based programming to Computer Science Priciples students using the programming language Python. It provides an overview of the tools available in Python, such as the basic commands, data types, variables, conditional statements, functions, loops, and simple data structures. At the end of the unit, students will have the skills that will help them develop programs where they will show what they learned in the unit.

This unit could serve as a final unit for the AP Computer Science course and be introduced right after the AP Exam as a transition from a block-based programming language such as Snap! to a text-based programming language.

USACO Problems

USACO (United States of America Computing Olympiad). It is a programming competition in the US in which many high school students participate.

I assign two USACO problems to students to have them practice their problem-solving and programming skills.

I gave the students the following directions:

  • Read the problem multiple times
  • Understand the core of the problem
  • Think on a possible solution
  • Create and outline for their algorithm
  • Implement their algorithm
