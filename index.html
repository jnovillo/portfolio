About me

Hello, welcome to my Professional Portfolio. My name is Jessica Novillo Argudo. I am passionate about Computer Science and Education.

I hold a bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Universidad de Cuenca in Ecuador. I have been working in New York for fourteen years as a software developer in the finance industry. I have worked on developing websites, assisting quantitative analysts in developing programs that use financial models, managing large databases and creating reports for clients.

I am currently compleating my final semester of a Master of Computer Science Education (K-12th) at Hunter College in New York, NY.

I have always had the dream to change my career and become an educator to teach students what I have learned throughout my professional career. Also, I have seen New York willing to make Computer Science accessible to students of all ages (K-12th grade). This was another reason that motivated me and made me realize that it was time to get into the education field.

I am confident that my skills, all the education knowledge I have gained, and my passion for teaching would make me a good teacher. I am eager to inspire new generations to immerse themselves in this modern world full of technology that is constantly evolving.

Introduction to programming in Python

This unit introduces text-based programming to Computer Science Priciples students using the programming language Python. It provides an overview of the tools available in Python, such as the basic commands, data types, variables, conditional statements, functions, loops, and simple data structures. At the end of the unit, students will have the skills that will help them develop programs where they will show what they learned in the unit.

This unit could serve as a final unit for the AP Computer Science course and be introduced right after the AP Exam as a transition from a block-based programming language such as Snap! to a text-based programming language.
